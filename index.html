Neat English [also called Waynwood English] is an accent made to be as clear and precise as possible, based somewhat on the transatlantic accent with some tweaks [mainly being that it is fully rhotic]. Here is its info:

The grapheme 'er' is always pronounced /əɹ/ (which I'll be typing as 'ɚ' after this point)

The grapheme 'r' is always pronounced /ɹ/

The grapheme 'l' is always pronounced /l/

/t/ is never softened to /d/, /ʔ/, or /ɾ/ (the same can be said about /d/ on the last two)

/ð/ is never softened to /d/ or /v/

/θ/ is never softened to /t/ or /f/

/ɛ/ is pronounced /ɛ~e/

/o/ (or /ɒ/) is always pronounced /o/

/əʊ/ (or /oʊ/) is always pronounced /əʊ/

There is no Rosas - roses merger, meaning 'Rosas' is /ɹəʊzəz/ and 'roses' is /ɹəʊzɪz/

There is no Mary - marry - merry merger, meaning 'Mary' is /mɛːɹi/, 'marry' is /mæɹi/, and 'merry' is /mɛɹi/

There is no father - bother merger, meaning 'father' is /fɑːðɚ/ and 'bother' is /boðɚ/

There is no trap - bath merger, meaning 'trap' is /træp/ and 'bath' is /bɑːθ/

There is no wine - whine merger, meaning 'wine' is /waɪn/ and 'whine' is /ʍaɪn/

There is no mirror - nearer merger, meaning 'mirror' is /mɪɹɚ/ and 'nearer' is /niːɹɚ/

And, as a personal preference, iron is pronounced /aɪɹɪn/
